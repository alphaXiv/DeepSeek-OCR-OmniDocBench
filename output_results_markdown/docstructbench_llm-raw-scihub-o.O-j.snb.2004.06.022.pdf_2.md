The main goal of this paper is to investigate the electric resistance of a layered phthalocyanine and palladium (Pc + Pd) structure under the influence of various hydrogen concentrations in nitrogen and air. We show that during the

interaction of hydrogen with bilayer structures metal-free phthalocyanine and palladium there occurs a strong change in the resistance of these structures. This would correspond to an acousto-electric effect in the SAW sensor system

Fig. 3 displays the changes in electrical resistance (R) over time (Time [s]) for two different bilayer structures exposed to hydrogen gas ( $ext{H}_2$ ) in a nitrogen atmosphere, measured at  $T hicksim 25^ ext{o} ext{C}$  and a flow rate  $Q = 1000 ext{ ml/min}$ .

The two structures measured are:

- 55 nm  $ext{H}_2 ext{Pc}$  + 10 nm Pd (represented by circles, measured against the left R [ $ext{\Omega}$ ] axis).
- 80 nm  $ext{H}_2 ext{Pc}$  + 10 nm Pd (represented by triangles, measured against the right R [ $ext{\Omega}$ ] axis).

(a) shows the response to lower hydrogen concentrations (0.5% to 2.5%  $ext{H}_2$  in nitrogen). The resistance of both structures increases sharply upon exposure to  $ext{H}_2$  and returns to baseline when the gas is removed. The 55 nm structure shows a larger absolute resistance change (from ~5400  $ext{\Omega}$  to ~5800  $ext{\Omega}$  at 1.5% and above) compared to the 80 nm structure (from ~1900  $ext{\Omega}$  to ~2200  $ext{\Omega}$  at 2.5%).

(b) shows the response to higher hydrogen concentrations (2.5% to 4%  $ext{H}_2$  in nitrogen). Both structures maintain a high resistance response plateau during exposure, with the 55 nm structure showing resistance around 5700  $ext{\Omega}$  and the 80 nm structure showing resistance around 1950  $ext{\Omega}$  (right axis).
