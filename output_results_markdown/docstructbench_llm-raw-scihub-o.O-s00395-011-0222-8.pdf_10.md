Figure 5 displays the effect of Akt overexpression (AOE) on aging-induced changes in components of the insulin signaling pathway in mice, comparing four groups: WT-Young, WT-Old, AOE-Young, and AOE-Old. Each panel includes representative Western blots (Insets) and corresponding bar graphs showing protein ratios (Mean  $\pm$  SEM, n = 5–7 mice per group).

- **Panel (A): IR $\beta$ -to- $\alpha$ -Tubulin Ratio.** Shows expression of Insulin Receptor  $\beta$  subunit (IR $\beta$ , 95KD) normalized to  $\alpha$ -Tubulin (55KD). WT-Old mice show significantly increased IR $\beta$  expression compared to WT-Young (\* $p < 0.05$ ). AOE-Young and AOE-Old groups show IR $\beta$  levels similar to WT-Young.
- **Panel (B): pPDK1-to- $\alpha$ -Tubulin Ratio.** Shows phosphorylation of PDK1 (58KD) normalized to  $\alpha$ -Tubulin. Both AOE-Young and AOE-Old groups show significantly increased pPDK1 levels compared to WT-Young (\* $p < 0.05$ ). WT-Old levels are slightly lower than WT-Young.
- **Panel (C): pAkt(Ser $^{473}$ )-to-Akt Ratio.** Shows Akt phosphorylation at Ser $^{473}$  (60KD) normalized to total Akt (60KD). WT-Old mice show significantly increased pAkt(Ser $^{473}$ ) compared to WT-Young (\* $p < 0.05$ ). Both AOE-Young and AOE-Old groups show significantly increased pAkt(Ser $^{473}$ ) compared to WT-Young (\* $p < 0.05$ ) and significantly increased compared to WT-Old ( $^{\#}$  $p < 0.05$ ).
- **Panel (D): pAkt(Thr $^{308}$ )-to-Akt Ratio.** Shows Akt phosphorylation at Thr $^{308}$  (60KD) normalized to total Akt. WT-Old levels are similar to WT-Young. Both AOE-Young and AOE-Old groups show significantly increased pAkt(Thr $^{308}$ ) compared to WT-Young (\* $p < 0.05$ ) and significantly increased compared to WT-Old ( $^{\#}$  $p < 0.05$ ).
- **Panel (E): pGSK3 $\beta$ -to-GSK3 $\beta$  Ratio.** Shows GSK3 $\beta$  phosphorylation (46KD) normalized to total GSK3 $\beta$  (46KD). No significant differences are indicated between groups.
- **Panel (F): pPTEN-to-PTEN Ratio.** Shows PTEN phosphorylation (54KD) normalized to total PTEN (54KD). WT-Old, AOE-Young, and AOE-Old groups all show significantly decreased pPTEN levels compared to WT-Young (\* $p < 0.05$ ). AOE-Old is also significantly decreased compared to WT-Old ( $^{\#}$  $p < 0.05$ ).
