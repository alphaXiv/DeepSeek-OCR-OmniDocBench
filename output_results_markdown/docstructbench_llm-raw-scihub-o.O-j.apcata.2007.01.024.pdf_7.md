As listed in Table 2, the addition of  $\text{NiSO}_4$  to  $\text{TiO}_2\text{--}\text{ZrO}_2$  also caused the acidity of  $\text{NiSO}_4/\text{TiO}_2\text{--}\text{ZrO}_2$  to increase considerably compared to  $\text{TiO}_2\text{--}\text{ZrO}_2$ , because the addition of  $\text{NiSO}_4$  to  $\text{TiO}_2\text{--}\text{ZrO}_2$  generates new acid sites by the inductive effect of sulfate species bonded to  $\text{TiO}_2\text{--}\text{ZrO}_2$  [17,35,47]. Namely, the number of acid sites for  $20\text{NiSO}_4/\text{TiO}_2$  and  $20\text{NiSO}_4/\text{ZrO}_2$  is 172 and 215  $\mu\text{mol}/\text{g}$ , respectively, while that of acid sites for  $20\text{NiSO}_4/25\text{TiO}_2\text{--}75\text{ZrO}_2$ ,  $20\text{NiSO}_4/50\text{TiO}_2\text{--}50\text{ZrO}_2$ , and  $20\text{NiSO}_4/75\text{TiO}_2\text{--}25\text{ZrO}_2$  is 261, 356, and 320  $\mu\text{mol}/\text{g}$ , respectively, showing a maximum acidity for  $20\text{NiSO}_4/50\text{TiO}_2\text{--}50\text{ZrO}_2$ .

We examined the effect of  $\text{TiO}_2\text{--}\text{ZrO}_2$  composition on the catalytic activity for ethylene dimerization. The catalytic activities for several catalysts against reaction time are plotted in Fig. 10. It is clear that the catalytic activities of three  $\text{NiSO}_4$  catalysts supported on binary oxide  $\text{TiO}_2\text{--}\text{ZrO}_2$  ( $2.6\text{--}3.7\ \text{mmol}/\text{g}$  5 min) are remarkably higher than those of  $\text{NiSO}_4$  catalysts supported on single oxide,  $20\text{NiSO}_4/\text{TiO}_2$  and  $20\text{NiSO}_4/\text{ZrO}_2$ . This can be explained if the number of acid sites of  $\text{TiO}_2\text{--}\text{ZrO}_2$  supported catalysts is considerably larger than those of  $\text{TiO}_2$ - or  $\text{ZrO}_2$ -supported catalysts,  $20\text{NiSO}_4/\text{TiO}_2$  or  $20\text{NiSO}_4/\text{ZrO}_2$ , as seen in Table 2. It has been reported that the catalytic activity for ethylene dimerization is closely correlated with the acidity of the catalyst [4,10,35]. Therefore, we conclude that the effect of  $\text{TiO}_2\text{--}\text{ZrO}_2$  composition is related to an increase in the number of surface acidic sites and to the increased surface area of  $\text{TiO}_2\text{--}\text{ZrO}_2$ -supported catalysts. Within supported  $\text{NiSO}_4$  catalysts, the catalytic activity for ethylene dimerization is in the order:  $20\text{NiSO}_4/50\text{TiO}_2\text{--}50\text{ZrO}_2 > 20\text{NiSO}_4/75\text{TiO}_2\text{--}25\text{ZrO}_2 > 20\text{NiSO}_4/25\text{TiO}_2\text{--}75\text{ZrO}_2 > 20\text{NiSO}_4/\text{ZrO}_2 > 20\text{NiSO}_4/\text{TiO}_2$ .

### 3.6. Correlation between catalytic activity and acidity

As mentioned above, the active site responsible for dimerization is suggested to consist of a low valent nickel ion and an acid, as observed in the nickel-containing catalyst [4,17,18]. The term 'low valent nickel' originated from the fact that the  $\text{NiO}\text{--}\text{SiO}_2$  catalyst was drastically poisoned by carbon monoxide, since a low valent nickel is favorable to chemisorb carbon monoxide [47,48]. Previously, from the results of IR of CO adsorbed on  $\text{NiSO}_4/\gamma\text{-Al}_2\text{O}_3$  and XPS to identify low valent nickel species, we reported that the active sites responsible for ethylene dimerization consist of a low-valent nickel,  $\text{Ni}^+$  and an acid [47]. Also, it was reported that the adsorption site of CO, that is, a low-valent nickel is responsible for the adsorption site of ethylene. Therefore, it is concluded that a low-valent nickel,  $\text{Ni}^+$ , plays the role of an adsorption site for ethylene, while acidic sites are responsible for the formation of reaction intermediates, such as ethyl cations [4,47]. Kazansky et al. concluded that  $\text{Ni}^+$  ions in  $\text{NiCa-Y}$  zeolite are responsible for the catalytic activity, using EPR spectroscopy to identify low valent nickel species [15]. Therefore, the nickel species should be active sites for ethylene dimerization. In fact,  $\text{TiO}_2\text{--}\text{ZrO}_2$  without  $\text{NiSO}_4$  is inactive for ethylene dimerization, as shown in Fig. 10.

It is known that for ethylene dimerization the variations in catalytic activities are closely correlated to the acidity values of catalyst [3,35]. The acidity values of several samples after evacuation at  $400^\circ\text{C}$  are listed in Table 2 together with their surface areas. Table 2 shows that the catalytic activities substantially run parallel to the acidity values. The catalytic activities of  $20\text{NiSO}_4/\text{TiO}_2$ ,  $20\text{NiSO}_4/\text{ZrO}_2$ , and  $\text{NiSO}_4/\text{TiO}_2\text{--}\text{ZrO}_2$  were examined; the results are shown as a function of acidity in Fig. 11, where catalysts were evacuated at  $500^\circ\text{C}$  for 1 h before reaction. It is confirmed that the catalytic activity gives a maximum at  $20\text{NiSO}_4/50\text{TiO}_2\text{--}\text{ZrO}_2$ . This seems to be correlated to the specific surface area and to the acidity of the catalysts. The acidity of catalysts calcined at  $500^\circ\text{C}$  was determined by the amount of  $\text{NH}_3$  irreversibly adsorbed at  $230^\circ\text{C}$  [3,4,6,9,38]. As shown in Fig. 11, the higher the acidity, the higher the catalytic activity. In this way it is demonstrated that the catalytic activity of supported  $\text{NiSO}_4$  catalysts essentially runs parallel to the acidity. Good correlations have been found in many cases between the acidity and the catalytic activities of solid acids. It has been reported that the catalytic activity values of nickel-containing catalysts in ethylene dimerization as well as in butene isomerization are closely correlated with the acidity of the catalysts [3,4,6,35]. Up to now, a correlation between catalytic activity for ethylene dimerization and acidity has been studied using supported  $\text{NiSO}_4$  [9,35,47], where only one kind of support material such as  $\text{Al}_2\text{O}_3$ ,  $\text{TiO}_2\text{--}\text{ZrO}_2$ , and  $\text{Al}_2\text{O}_3\text{--}\text{ZrO}_2$  was used. However, in this work we used different supports:  $\text{TiO}_2$ ,  $\text{ZrO}_2$  and three kinds of  $\text{TiO}_2\text{--}\text{ZrO}_2$ . In this case of  $\text{NiSO}_4$  supported on different materials, there was also a correlation between catalytic activity for ethylene dimerization and acidity, regardless of the kind of support ( $\text{TiO}_2$ ,  $\text{ZrO}_2$ , and  $\text{TiO}_2\text{--}\text{ZrO}_2$ ).
